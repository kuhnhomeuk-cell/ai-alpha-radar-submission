How signals are scored
What the dashboard is looking for

Cross-source confidence

A signal gets stronger when it appears in more than one place. For example, a topic that shows up in research papers, repositories, and community discussions is more trustworthy than a topic that only appears in one feed.

confidence = sources confirming the topic / active sources

Opportunity signals

Opportunity signals are topics with strong movement but low crowding. The dashboard looks for subjects that are accelerating, still niche enough to own, and showing real builder interest before they become obvious.

opportunity = 0.40*momentum + 0.35*(1 - saturation) + 0.25*builder signal + 0.20*venue signal

Signal score

The main score combines momentum, opportunity potential, lifecycle stage, and cross-source confirmation. Higher scores mean the topic is moving now and still has room for a creator to explain it clearly.

score = 100 * (0.35*momentum + 0.30*opportunity + 0.20*lifecycle + 0.15*confidence)

Saturation

Saturation estimates how crowded the topic already is. A lower saturation number usually means more room for a useful video, guide, or product angle. A higher number means the market may already be noisy.

saturation = 0.35*GitHub + 0.30*HN + 0.20*arXiv + 0.15*Semantic Scholar
